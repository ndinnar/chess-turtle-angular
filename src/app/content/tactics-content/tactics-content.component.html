Chess Tactics

What Are Chess Tactics?

Chess tactics are a move or a series of moves that give a player an advantage in the game. A tactic might limit the way a player's pieces can move, or lead to the capture of a piece. Tatics are typically short-term plans, as opposed to strategy, which is typically a larger ideas. But, just because tactics are short-term plans, does mean they are important. In fact, tactics are how most games are won or lost, especially in the beginner and intermediate levels of chess. As a beginning player, the one thing that will give you the biggest edge over your opponents is learning about tactics and how to spot them.

Types of Tactics

There are several different kinds of tactics that are used in chess. Here are a list of some of the most important ones to learn when you are first starting out. Click on any of the following tactics to learn even more about that tactic and practice using it:

Forks
A piece attacks two or more pieces at the same time.
Skewers
A threatened piece must step out of the way, allowing the piece behind it to be captured.
Pins
A piece prevents another piece from moving.
Revealed Attacks
A piece moves and reveals a surprise attack by another piece.
Sacrifices
Intentionally losing a piece to gain a large advantage or force a checkmate.
Desperados
A piece that is about to get captured goes down fighting.
X-Ray Attacks
A piece threatens the piece behind the piece that they are about to capture.
Interference
A piece step in front of a more important piece to block an attack.
Attraction
A piece forces another pices to come to a specific square, usually as part of a sacrifice.
Removing A Defender
Capturing a piece that is defending another piece.
Forcing A Stalemate
In a situation where you are probably going to lose, you can force the game to end in a draw instead.
Zwischenzug (In-Between Move)
Making a move before making a capture, retreating, or defending.
Zugswang (Forced Move)
A forced bad move because there are no other legal moves.
