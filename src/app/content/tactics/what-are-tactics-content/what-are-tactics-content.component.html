Chess Tactics: What Are Chess Tactics?

Chess tactics are a move or a series of moves that give a player an advantage in the game. A tactic might limit the way a player's pieces can move, or lead to the capture of a piece. Tatics are typically short-term plans, as opposed to strategy, which is typically a larger ideas. But, just because tactics are short-term plans, does mean they are important. In fact, tactics are how most games are won or lost, especially in the beginner and intermediate levels of chess. As a beginning player, the one thing that will give you the biggest edge over your opponents is learning about tactics and how to spot them.

Types of Tactics

There are several different kinds of tactics that are used in chess. Here are a list of some of the most important ones to learn when you are first starting out. Click on any of the following tactics to learn even more about that tactic and practice using it:

  • Forks - This is when a piece attacks two or more pieces at the same time.
  • Skewers - A skewer is when a threatened piece must step out of the way, allowing the piece behind it to be captured.
  • Pins - A pin is when a piece prevents another piece from moving, either because it would be an illegal move (it puts the king in check) it would cause the loss of another, more important piece (like the queen).
  • Revealed Attacks - A revealed attack is when one of your pieces moves, and reveals a surprise attack by another one of your pieces.
  • Sacrifices - In a sacrifice, you are intentionally losing a piece, usually to gain a large advantage or force a checkmate.
  • Desperados - In this tactic, a piece that is about to get captured goes down fighting and captures a piece itself, sometimes of lesser value, but still ends up getting captured.
  • X-Ray Attacks - X-ray attacks threaten the piece behind the piece that they are about to capture.
  • Interference - This tactic has a piece step in front of a more important piece to block and attack.
  • Attraction - Attraction is when a piece forces another pices to come to a specific square, usually as part of a sacrifice.
  • Removing A Defender - This is when you make plans to capture a piece that is defending another of your opponent's pieces.
  • Forcing A Stalemate - When you are in a situation where you are probably going to lose, sometimes you can force the game to end in a draw instead.
  • Zwischenzug (In-Between Move) - Sometimes, you don'thave to make a move right away. This tactic is about making a move before making a capture or using a tactic.
  • Zugswang (Forced Move) - This is a player is forced to make a bad move because they have no other legal move.
